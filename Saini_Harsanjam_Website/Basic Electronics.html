Engineering Notation


Engineering notation is a variation of scientific notation. In electronics and many other scientific and technical fields, there are certain powers of 10 that are used more often than others. Names have been given to certain powers of 10 to make them even easier to use. The names of the powers are givem in a multiple of three: 103, 106, 109 , 10-3, 10-6, 10-9. When converting numbers to engineering notation they should be written using SI prefixes and units to 2 decimal points.

Electron Theory

Electrons


They are particles around nucleus of atom

They are lighter in weight

They carry electric (negative) charge

They move from one atom to another atom within same element, which creates electricity
Ohm's Law

In an electrical circuit whose conductance (resistance) never varies, the current is directly proportional to the applied voltage

If you double the voltage, you double the current

If you cut the voltage in half, the current goes down by half

Calculations


V = IR

I = V/R

R = V/I
Series Circuit

The series circuit is a circuit with one loop, where the current travels in one path. Series circuits contain all of the basic circuit components (load, resistors, source). In a series circuit, the current remains constant throughout the ciruit and the voltage drops in each resistor.

Parallel Circuits

A parallel circuit is a circuit with multiple loops for the current to travel. Series circuits contain all of the basic circuit components (load, resistors, source). In a parallel circuit, the current splits among the individual branches of the circuit and the voltage remains constant throughout the circuit.

Series-Parallel Circuit

The series-parallel circuits have aspects of both series and parallel circuits. They also contain all of the basic circuit components (load, resistors, source).
